Guillaume Caron
連携研究副ラボ長 — 個人ウェブサイト

経歴

Guillaume Caron is co-director of CNRS-AIST Joint Robotics Laboratory (JRL), IRL, Japan since 2022. He joined JRL as CNRS delegate in 2019. Since 2011, he has been Associate Professor at Universite de Picardie Jules Verne (UPJV), MIS laboratory, France, where he was heading from 2016 to 2020 the Robotic Perception group. He received the Ph.D. degree in robotics and the Habilitation degree from UPJV in 2010 and 2019, respectively. He was a postdoc fellow at Inria Rennes/IRISA, France (2010-2011) and a visiting research scholar at the University of Osaka, Japan (Apr.-May 2013). His research interests include artificial vision for robotics, real-time visual 3D tracking and servoing and digital heritage. He has been associate editor for the IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters since 2023. He has been the vice-chair of the IAPR TC 19 on "Computer vision for cultural heritage applications" since 2025 after being the chair from 2018 to 2022 (2 terms). He organized several international workshops related to heritage applications in conjunction with major intertnational conferences in robotics (IEEE/RSJ IROS 2021) and computer vision (IEEE/CVF ICCV 2017, 2019, 2023).
